## #moe — MoE Training Hits Its Stride

Big week for mixture-of-experts. willheld and Kaiyue-Wen converged on **linear decay over WSD** for the learning rate schedule, yielding a [0.03 BPB improvement](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1365044508546568372/1485898126454820907) at 3e18 flops. Larry gave props to Pranshu Chaturvedi for early QB load balancing work and willheld for optimizing the implementation — it's now working across scales with [zero hyperparameters and a 10-15% speedup](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1365044508546568372/1487197835580412055). dlwh and Larry are testing capacity fraction, with 0.8 [surprisingly not hurting loss](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1365044508546568372/1486881651421089812) ("I know I was joking before that it was just dropout but like it kinda is"). Larry kicked off the [1e22 MoE run](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1365044508546568372/1487693945944412170) (35B/A5B, 326B tokens) on v4-512, expected to complete in a week — early results are [close behind the 1e23 Delphi dense run](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1365044508546568372/1487889648859480217).

## #scaling-laws — Chinchilla Paper Published, Forecasting Work

Eric Czech published the Chinchilla scaling law analysis preprint: [arxiv.org/abs/2603.22339](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22339), with a [project site, HF dataset, and Claude demo](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1356490712199462912/1486394546580623381). Kaiyue-Wen provided a [helpful breakdown](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1356490712199462912/1486553066202464336) of the competing batch size scaling theories (alpha = 1/2 vs 2/3 vs empirical 0.57), concluding it doesn't matter much in practice. dlwh had his golem bot [file an issue](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1356490712199462912/1486434203661766816) to track an agent-curated summary of latest scaling heuristics (marin-community/marin#4147).

willheld is working on [predicting final loss from early loss](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1356490712199462912/1487571716833022084) — single early loss point works better than multiple, but adding model size and token volume to the regression helps. Percy Liang is interested in modeling Loss(N, D) across different ratios to extrapolate.

## #data-curation — Synthetic Data Strategy

willheld is driving a push for **cheap, CPU-only synthetic data generation** for arithmetic, regex, bash commands, and other core capabilities — motivated by the observation that the 32B model [struggled on basic arithmetic](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1368297424086499359/1486977555842994246) despite doing well on harder math benchmarks. The pitch: use the [~50k idle CPUs](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1368297424086499359/1486985213639917640) on training machines to generate O(1T) tokens deterministically. Open question is [how many tokens of deterministically-generated data are useful](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1368297424086499359/1487223846929961040) before overfitting to format. nato pointed out [Dolma midtraining datasets](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1368297424086499359/1487266445833404516) already include some of this.

Benjamin Feuer shared the [OpenThoughts-3 dataset with GLM 4.7](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1368297424086499359/1485949669468012575) and surfaced several large math datasets, though willheld is [cautious about unknown-provenance datasets](https://marin-discord.pages.dev/#1368297424086499359/1487217376335233042) due to poisoning risk.
